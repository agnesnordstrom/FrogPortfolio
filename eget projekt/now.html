Where do frogs live?

Frog Habitats

Frogs inhabit various ecosystems across the globe, from lush rainforests to vast deserts.

Wetlands and marshes

Frogs often inhabit wetlands and marshes, thanks to the abundant water supply. These areas provide ideal breeding grounds and a reliable source of food for frogs. Standing water offers protection for eggs and tadpoles from predators.

→

Rainforests and Tropical Forests

in lush rainforests, frogs exhibit vibrant colors and patterns, serving both as camouflage and a warning to potential predators . The leaf litter, streams, and puddles provide ample hiding spots and breeding sites.

Desert Oases

Certain frog species can survive in deserts by residing near oases where underground water sources emerge. During dry periods, they burrow underground and emerge when water becomes available.

Temperate Forests and Woodlands

Temperate forests and woodlands host a variety of frogs. These habitats offer a mix of terrestrial and aquatic features. Frogs may breed in ponds, streams, or rain-filled depressions and use leaf litter for shelter.

Urban and Suburban Areas

Frogs have adapted to urban and suburban environments, utilizing ponds, ditches, and stormwater drains. While they face challenges like pollution and habitat destruction, some species coexist with human development.

Caves and Underground

Uniquely, some frog species thrive underground in caves and chambers. These creatures have evolved to survive in subterranean darkness.

Useful Frog terminology

Amphibian
cold-blooded vertebrate of the class Amphibia, comprising frogs, toads, newts, salamanders and caecilians. Typically the larvae are aquatic (dependent on water) and breathe using gills whereas the adults are terrestrial or semi-terrestrial (land based), breathing by lungs and/or through their moist skin.
Anuran
Amphibians without tails (i.e. frogs and toads).
Froglet
young frog that has just completed its metamorphosis from a tadpole and left the pond.
Metamorphosis
a profound change in form from one life stage to the next e.g. a tadpole developing into a frog.
Spawn
amphibian eggs.
Tadpole
the aquatic young of amphibians, particularly frogs and toads.
Toadlet
young toad that has just completed its metamorphosis from a tadpole and left the pond.
Toadpole
sometimes used to describe toad tadpoles.
